HR Compensation Tool

For this project I created a model that shows the pay for three different tiers of employees and compares that pay to benchmark data. The model can also be used to show performance based on each individual employee, to see which people are either meeting or exceeding standards.
View HR Compensation Tool

First Capstone

Here I had a dataset of 4,000 cars and 100,000 transactions for a car rental company in 2018. I was tasked with finding a way to maximize their profit with the existing data. In this model I created several strategies to see how they would affect the data. In my third strategy, I identified "problem cars" that weren't making the company much money and were costing a lot to maintain. After removing 42 of these cars from the company's inventory, they gained approximately $40,000 in overall revenue!
View First Capstone

Second Capstone

For this project I had a dataset with information on the housing industry. I needed to use statistical analysis to find the strongest factor when identifying a house that would sell for the most money. I used regression to find a strong correlation between overall price and overall quality of the houses. I then ran some t-tests to show that houses in two specific neighborhoods had the highest probability of having a high quality home, and therefore a higher price.
View Second Capstone

Third Capstone

In my third and final capstone I used Python to access a dataset on movies. The set contained information on three decades of film, spanning from 1986 to 2016. I used PANDAS to build and manipulate DataFrames out of the data and ran some t-tests, eventually discovering that animated movies are the most profitable genre of film. I also used t tests to find out that horror films are making about triple the profit than they were making three decades ago!
